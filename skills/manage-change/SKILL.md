# Change Management Plan

You are creating a Stilla canvas for an organisational change initiative. Structure the canvas to guide the team through planning, communicating, and tracking adoption of the change.

## Canvas Structure

Format the canvas content using these sections in order:

### Overview

Summarise the change in 2–3 sentences. What is changing, why is it changing, and what does success look like? Include the target go-live or rollout date if known.

### Stakeholder analysis

Identify the key groups and individuals affected by or involved in the change. For each stakeholder group capture:
- **Role** — sponsor, champion, impacted user, decision-maker, or resister
- **Impact level** — high, medium, or low
- **Concerns or needs** — what matters most to them

### Communication plan

Define what to communicate, to whom, and when. For each communication action capture:
- **Audience** — who receives it
- **Message** — core message or theme
- **Channel** — Slack, email, all-hands, 1:1, etc.
- **Timing** — date or milestone trigger
- **Owner** — who sends it

### Training and enablement

Describe what training or support people need to adopt the change. Include:
- Training format (workshop, written guide, video, shadowing, etc.)
- Who delivers it and who receives it
- Timeline and any prerequisites

### Risks and mitigations

List the top risks that could slow or derail adoption. For each:
- **Risk** — what could go wrong
- **Likelihood** — high, medium, or low
- **Mitigation** — what action reduces or removes the risk
- **Owner** — who is responsible for the mitigation

### Milestones and timeline

List the key dates and milestones for the rollout. Keep these to the most important checkpoints (announcement, pilot, go-live, review).

### Adoption tracking

Define how success will be measured. Capture:
- Key metrics or KPIs (e.g. activation rate, support ticket volume, NPS)
- Baseline (current state) and target
- How and when metrics will be reviewed
- Who owns the tracking

## Formatting Guidelines

- Use **bold** for labels within bullet points
- Use bullet points within each section
- Keep the overview and risk lists scannable — avoid long paragraphs
- Tag owners by name where possible so action items are clear
- Add concrete follow-up tasks as Stilla action items with assignees and due dates, not inline text
- If a section is not yet known (e.g. training plan is TBD), note it as "To be defined" rather than omitting the section
